Start your timeline

Add a tumor below, then enter the measurements from your scan reports. Each tumor gets its own chart, and the medications you record are shaded behind every chart — so you can see, at a glance, what changed while you were on what.

  1. Add a tumor and choose how it's measured — a single diameter, two diameters, or a volume.
  2. Enter measurements from each scan, with the scan date.
  3. Record medications and important events to overlay them on the charts.

The chart below is just an example — it disappears as soon as you add your own first tumor.

Tumors

One entry per tumor. If your clinic changes measurement method, add it as a new tumor (e.g. “Left VS — volumetric”).

Medications

Shaded behind every chart. Leave the end date empty for a medication you're still on. A dose change is just a new period with the same name — each dose gets its own subtle shade.

Comment on your experience with this medication — side effects, how you're tolerating it, or anything else worth remembering.

Events

Moments worth marking on the timeline — a surgery, radiation, a diagnosis. Drawn as a thin line on every chart, or only on one tumor's chart if you pick it.

Share anonymously

Put a copy of this dataset — identified only by its Patient ID — on the public gallery so other patients can see a real course over time. Nothing syncs automatically: you choose when to push an update.
